# Investment Scoring System (Compounder Model)

This system converts qualitative investment analysis into a structured scoring model.

---

# TOTAL SCORE = 100

## 1. Business Quality (20 points)

- Clear business model (0–5)
- Pricing power (0–5)
- Scalability (0–5)
- Revenue visibility (0–5)

---

## 2. Moat Strength (20 points)

- Network effects
- Switching cost
- Brand strength
- Cost advantage

Scoring:
- 5 = extremely strong, expanding
- 3 = stable
- 1 = weak or fading

---

## 3. Growth Quality (15 points)

- Revenue CAGR (3–5 years)
- Earnings growth consistency
- Market expansion potential

---

## 4. Financial Quality (15 points)

- ROIC trend
- Free cash flow consistency
- Margin stability
- Debt level

---

## 5. Valuation (15 points)

- Historical valuation percentile
- PEG ratio
- FCF yield
- DCF upside

Scoring rule:
- Undervalued = high score
- Fair = medium
- Overvalued = low

---

## 6. Trend (Right-side Entry) (10 points)

- Weekly trend
- Monthly trend
- Volume confirmation
- Base formation

---

## 7. Risk (Negative scoring impact -15 to 0)

Deduct points for:

- Structural decline risk
- Regulatory risk
- High dilution
- Weak moat
- Industry disruption

---

# FINAL SCORE INTERPRETATION

## 85–100
Strong Compounder (A Tier)

## 70–84
High Quality (B Tier)

## 50–69
Speculative / Watchlist (C Tier)

## <50
Avoid (D Tier)

---

# FINAL OUTPUT FORMAT

Always return:

- Total Score: /100
- Tier: A/B/C/D
- 5-year 3x probability
- 5-year 5x probability
- Key upside driver
- Key risk factor
