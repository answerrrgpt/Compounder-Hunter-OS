# Investment Philosophy

## Core Objective
Identify long-term compounders capable of delivering 3–5x returns over a 3–5 year horizon with controlled risk of permanent capital loss.

---

## Core Beliefs

### 1. Quality > Cheapness
A cheap bad company is worse than a fairly priced great company.

### 2. Compounding > Timing
The goal is not to buy bottoms, but to hold long-term winners.

### 3. Moat Drives Returns
Sustainable competitive advantage is the primary driver of long-term returns.

### 4. Growth Must Be Durable
Prefer long-term compounding over short-term explosive growth.

---

## Investment Filters

- Strong and durable moat
- High ROIC (>15% preferred)
- Scalable business model
- Large addressable market (TAM expansion potential)
- Strong free cash flow generation

---

## Red Flags

- Declining ROIC
- Weak pricing power
- High dilution
- Weak capital allocation
- Structural industry decline

---

## Core Principle
Avoid permanent capital loss first. Maximize upside second.
